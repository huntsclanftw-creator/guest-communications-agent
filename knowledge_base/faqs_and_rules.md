# FAQs & House Rules — Serenity Cabins

This document contains common guest questions and the answers James (or Mandy) actually gives. Use these as a reference for how to respond — adapt to James's natural voice, don't copy robotically.

---

## Access & Directions

**Q: How do I get to the cabin? I can't find it on Google Maps.**
> "Drive past the main house and go straight. You will see a gravel road marked with reflective stakes. Follow the road and the cabin is at the end. A Google Pin Drop will be included in your check-in details."

**Q: Where is the entrance to Rokeby Road?**
> "Look carefully for the mailbox marked 1327; it is easy to miss on your first time visiting. Then turn into the gravel driveway and through the gates marked LV. The door with the smart lock is on the right side of the house, closest to the road — not the roundabout. Input your code and hit the checkmark to unlock."

**Q: I'm having trouble getting in / locked out.**
> Call/text James directly. Provide his number. This is urgent — respond immediately.

## Check-in / Check-out

**Q: What time is check-in?**
> Check-in is at 4:00 PM. It is contactless, so no need to wait for anyone — just use your door code.

**Q: Can I check in early?**
> "It is contactless check in so you can arrive as early as you want." (only if cabin has been cleaned — confirm before telling guests this)

**Q: What is check-out time?**
> 11:00 AM for most properties. 10:00 AM for Rokeby Road.

**Q: Can I have a late check-out?**
> "We can offer a complimentary late check-out of an hour, and then any extra time after that for $25/hour. If you're thinking past noon, it's actually easier to just book an extra day — I can discount it for you."

**Q: My booking said check-in is tomorrow but I booked for today.**
> "Yes, it is an automatic message that is supposed to go out 24 hours prior, but since you booked less than that it still sent. It is ready for you today!"

## Sleeping Arrangements

**Q: How many people can stay?**
> Serenity Cabin sleeps up to 4 comfortably. There is a queen bed in the upstairs loft and a full-size sleeper sofa in the living room. We can also provide a twin inflatable mattress if necessary, but more than 5 people in the cabin becomes really cramped.

**Q: What is the second bed? I only see 1 listed.**
> "Our sleeping arrangements are a queen bed in the upstairs loft and a full-size sleeper sofa in the living room."

## Appliances & Cabin Issues

**Q: The Keurig isn't working / water isn't coming out.**
> "Sometimes they get clogged with some coffee grind that washes back up. Try running it without a k-cup a few times until it runs clear and that should fix it!"

**Q: The AC isn't cooling the cabin fast enough.**
> "Setting the unit lower than 68°F won't cause it to cool down quicker but can actually cause it to freeze over and stop working. The best way to cool everything is to promote air circulation using the box fan and living room fans. The central unit was just inspected and got a clean bill of health, so it should start cooling off."

**Q: Is there hot water in the shower?**
> "Just more patience — the hot water takes a moment to travel up."

**Q: There are ants in the kitchen.**
> "We have placed some ant bait out. We haven't had time to get our pest control out for a more in-depth treatment. The best thing is to keep food in containers and in the fridge, and use the cleaning spray in the downstairs closet."

**Q: The drain is slow.**
> "You can try boiling some water in the kettle and then pouring it down the drain. Our septic was great last time we had it inspected, but sometimes guests flush wipes that can clog it." (Note: advise guests not to flush wipes going forward)

**Q: The cabin smells.**
> "Try cracking a window to air it out."

## Damaged or Missing Items After Checkout

James's approach: Direct, not accusatory.

## Weather & Safety Proactive Alerts

James sends these unprompted when conditions occur:
- Severe thunderstorm: "We are in a severe thunderstorm warning right now. There are lighters on the island and flashlights in the side table next to the dining room."
- Tornado/severe wind: "Heads up that we have severe winds forecasted for tomorrow and potential tornadoes. This is crazy!"
- Bear sighting: (see Bear Safety above)
- Maintenance arriving: "The chimney contractors just told me they are arriving early soon to put a cap on the chimney to prevent birds from getting in the wood burning stove. They should not need to gain entry."
- Mowing: "Just a heads up that I will be mowing near the cabin later this afternoon!"
- Dinner party at main house: "Just a heads up that we are hosting a small dinner party at the main house so there may be some cars parking in the overflow lot by the cabin. They won't be here overnight."
