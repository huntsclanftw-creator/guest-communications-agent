# Guest communication tone guide
**Far Longing Getaways & Mars Hill Getaways — STR guest messaging**
*Draft v1 — language patterns to be refined once past guest messages are added to knowledge base*

---

## Core principles

1. Be genuinely warm — write like you're welcoming friends to your place, not processing a transaction.
2. Keep it concise and helpful. Get to the answer quickly, without unnecessary filler.
3. Let the cozy, rustic character of the properties come through naturally in your language — nothing overly polished or corporate.
4. Build a relationship. Every message is a small opportunity to make guests feel at home and want to come back.
5. Add a small personal touch to simple answers — a brief friendly line before or after — but don't overdo it.

---

## Scenario examples

### Wi-Fi password
**Guest asks:** "Hey, what's the Wi-Fi password?"

> Hey there! The Wi-Fi network is [NETWORK NAME] and the password is [PASSWORD]. Let me know if you have any trouble connecting — happy to help!

---

### Check-in confirmation
**Guest messages after booking to confirm check-in details.**

> So excited to have you! Your check-in is at [TIME] — the lockbox code is [CODE] and it's right by the front door. Settle in and make yourself at home. Can't wait for you to experience this place.

---

### Local recommendation
**Guest asks:** "Any recommendations for places to eat nearby?"

> We love sending folks to [RESTAURANT NAME] — great [food type] and a really local feel. It's about [X] minutes from you.

---

### Property/amenity question
**Guest asks:** "How do I get the hot tub going?"

> Great choice! The hot tub is on the back porch — just flip the switch on the panel and give it about 20 minutes to heat up. Enjoy it!

---

### Early check-in request
**Guest asks if they can check in before the listed time.**

> Love the enthusiasm! I'll check with our team and see what we can do — I'll get back to you as soon as I know. Fingers crossed we can make it work!

---
